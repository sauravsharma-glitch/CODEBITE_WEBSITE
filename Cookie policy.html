Cookie Policy

Last Updated: March 16, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how CodeBite uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our website. It explains what these technologies are and why we use them, as well as your rights to control our use of them.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several reasons:

  • Essential Operations: Some cookies are required for the website to function properly.
  • Performance and Analytics: We use cookies to understand how visitors interact with our site, which helps us improve the user experience.
  • Preferences: Cookies remember your settings and preferences to enhance your experience.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Essential Cookies Required for basic site functionality, including form submissions and navigation. Session / Persistent
Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics) Help us understand how visitors use our site, which pages are popular, and how users navigate the site. Up to 2 years
Preference Cookies Remember your preferences, such as language or region. 1 year
reCAPTCHA Cookies Google reCAPTCHA uses cookies to distinguish humans from bots for security purposes. Session

4. Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third-party cookies to report usage statistics of the service and deliver advertisements on and through the service. These include:

  • Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to collect information about how visitors use our site. Google Analytics collects information anonymously and generates reports of website trends. Google's Privacy Policy
  • Google reCAPTCHA: We use reCAPTCHA to protect our forms from spam and abuse. reCAPTCHA analyzes traffic to distinguish humans from bots. Google's Privacy Policy

5. How to Control Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences by:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View cookies stored on your device and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

To learn how to manage cookies in popular browsers, visit:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Cookie Consent Tool

When you first visit our site, we display a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. Your choice is stored for future visits.

6. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

If you disable or refuse cookies, some parts of our website may become inaccessible or not function properly. For example:

  • Essential cookies are required for form submission - if blocked, you cannot use our contact form
  • Analytics cookies help us improve the site - disabling them doesn't affect functionality but limits our ability to improve
